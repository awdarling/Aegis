# Aegis for Managers
### Run scheduling from your inbox

Aegis is your scheduling assistant. You can **email** it to send schedules, approve requests, find coverage, answer staffing questions, and make changes — all in plain English. It confirms before it changes anything, and it routes employee requests to you for approval.

**Email Aegis at:** `aegis@aegis.quriasolutions.com`

---

## What you can do

### Send the schedule to your staff
> *"Send this week's schedule to everyone."*

Every employee gets their shifts plus the full week's schedule.

### Approve or deny requests
When an employee requests **time off** or an **availability change**, Aegis emails you with the details and **Approve / Deny** buttons. Click one — or just reply **YES** or **NO**. The employee is told your decision automatically.

### Re-run a time-off check when requests pile up
When several time-off requests come in close together, Aegis's suggestion on an older one can get out of date — once you approve a few people, the coverage picture has changed. You can ask Aegis to **take a fresh look** at any pending request against everyone who's *currently* approved:

- In **Homebase**, open the **Time Off** tab and click **"Re-run check"** on the request. The suggestion refreshes right there.
- Or, right from the **email**, click **"Re-run check"** on the request's card. Aegis re-checks in the background and **replies in that same email thread** with an updated card a moment later — no new email to dig for.

Either way you get Aegis's latest read (approve, or don't, and whether approving now would leave a coverage gap) so you can decide with current information.

### Once it's decided, it's settled
The moment a request is approved or denied — by you, by another manager, in Homebase, or by email — Aegis drops a **"Resolved"** note into each manager's email thread for that request, and the Approve / Deny buttons stop working. So if two managers are looking at the same request, the second one to click sees a friendly note like *"already approved by Carolyn on Jun 17 — nothing changed"* instead of accidentally acting on it twice. And if you click an action link that's already been used, Aegis reassures you that **your earlier action went through and nothing was missed**.

### Find emergency coverage
When someone calls out, tell Aegis. It instantly gives you a ranked list of who can cover — qualified for the role, not already working, no time off, fewest hours first — with their contact info.

> *"Maisey can't come in today, I need coverage."*

Then you decide:
- Reply **ALL** to have Aegis message everyone on the list at once, or
- Reply a **name** (or a few names) to contact just them.

The first person to accept gets the shift; everyone else is told it's covered, and **the schedule updates automatically**. If no one accepts within 30 minutes, Aegis checks whether you'd like it to reach out to another batch — it never contacts more people without your go-ahead.

### Ask staffing questions
> *"Who's working Saturday?"*
> *"Who's available Saturday afternoon?"*
> *"How many lifeguards do I have on Friday night?"*
> *"Is anyone over 40 hours this week?"*

### Make changes by email
Aegis confirms each change before it saves, then you reply YES.

> *"Change Maisey's role to Headguard."*
> *"Set the max weekly hours to 40."*
> *"Maisey mentioned she can't work Fridays anymore — update her availability."*

That last one is handy: when an employee mentions something in passing, you can update it for them on the spot.

### Editing the schedule itself
Adding, moving, or removing shifts is still done in **Homebase** — if you ask Aegis to edit the schedule, it'll point you there. Everything else above, Aegis handles by email.

---

## A few tips
- **Write naturally** — Aegis understands plain language.
- **It always confirms** before saving a change or contacting staff.
- **Employee requests come to you** — nothing about pay, availability, or time off changes without your approval.
- Coverage and approvals are **reply-based** (YES/NO), which is exactly how they'll work when this moves to text message down the road.
